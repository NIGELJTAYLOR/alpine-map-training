Learning aim

Identify the three principal summit forms from contour spacing alone, and predict where the steepest descent line will run.

A summit, on a map, is the smallest closed contour. What lies beneath that single ring tells you most of what you need to know about the descent — long before you see the snow.

Three archetypes recur. Read them in this order:

Dome
Peak
Plateau

How the contour spacing reads

  • Dome — concentric rings, evenly spaced. Predictable; every aspect skis similarly.
  • Peak — rings tightening rapidly toward the summit. Steepest line varies by aspect.
  • Plateau — broad contour with sub-summits. Navigation hazard in poor visibility.

Learning aim

Identify the three principal summit forms from contour spacing and shape alone, and predict where the steepest descent line will run before committing to it on the ground.

A summit, on a map, is the smallest closed contour. What lies beneath that single ring tells you most of what you need to know about the descent — long before you see the snow. Read three archetypes, in order:

Dome · even spacing
Peak · tightening rings
Plateau · sub-summits

How the contour spacing reads

  • Dome — concentric rings, evenly spaced. Predictable, every aspect skis similarly. Watch for the convex roll where the spacing widens.
  • Peak — rings tightening rapidly toward the summit. Steepest line varies by aspect; read which side compresses first to find your fall line.
  • Plateau — a single broad contour with sub-summits. Navigation hazard in poor vis: no obvious fall line. Use bearings, not eyes.
