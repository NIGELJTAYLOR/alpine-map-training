### Exercise 1 - Read the bearing

Answers depend on the trainer's photographs. For trainer reference, the standard photographs are configured as:

| Photograph | Bearing |
|---|---|
| A | 047 degrees |
| B | 192 degrees |
| C | 318 degrees |

Mark within 2 degrees of the correct value. A candidate who reads to within 2 degrees has met the IMS / EMS working standard for reading accuracy. A candidate consistently 5 degrees or more off is reading the wrong index mark or has the compass tilted; coach the holding habits.

### Exercise 2 - Cardinal cross-check

For the standard photographs:

| Photograph | Closest cardinal | Angular difference |
|---|---|---|
| A | NE (045) | 2 degrees |
| B | S (180) | 12 degrees |
| C | NW (315) | 3 degrees |

Mark for the correct cardinal identification, not the angular arithmetic (the arithmetic is just a sanity check). A candidate who calls 192 degrees "south-west" has a four-direction-vs-eight-direction slip from C4.2; recycle that page if persistent.

### Exercise 3 - Holding the compass

A strong answer should name all three habits and give one concrete example of failure for each:

- **Hold the compass flat.** Failure example: a tilted compass lets the needle drag against the bezel; the bearing reads slow or sticks at the wrong figure.
- **Hold away from steel.** Failure example: a compass next to a phone reads several degrees off; the candidate walks on the wrong line.
- **Wait for the needle to settle.** Failure example: a candidate reads the bezel before the needle has stopped swinging and gets a different figure on the next reading.

Mark for the explicit pairing of habit and failure; a vague answer ("hold it properly") is not enough.

### Exercise 4 - Quick-check drill

There is no fixed answer; the drill produces the candidate's own reading. Mark by checking that:

- the candidate has stated the expected bearing for the known direction (for example, due east = 90 degrees),
- the candidate has noted the actual reading,
- and the candidate has identified at least one nearby object or condition that might explain any difference.

If the difference is more than 10 degrees and no source is identified, the compass itself may be faulty; suggest a re-test in a different location.

### Exercise 5 - When the needle is sluggish

Correct answers:

1. **Cold temperature.** Action: allow extra settling time (up to 10 seconds in temperatures well below freezing); warm the compass inside a glove if the needle is unusable.
2. **Magnetic interference.** Action: move the compass away from the suspect source (phone, ice axe, ski binding); re-read; if the bearing changes, the source is confirmed.
3. **Damaged compass.** Action: check for visible damage to the pivot or bezel; if the compass no longer settles reliably, replace it.

Mark by checking each cause is paired with the correct action. A candidate who names the cause but gives the wrong action ("cold temperature: replace the compass") has not internalised the troubleshooting hierarchy.
