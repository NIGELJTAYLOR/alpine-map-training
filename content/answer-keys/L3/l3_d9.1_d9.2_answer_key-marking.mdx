The D9.1 / D9.2 pair is about route-card production. After this pair the candidate has the basic card; D9.3 and D9.4 add the contingency layer.

The 20-minute speed standard is non-negotiable for IMS / EMS-level work. A candidate consistently above 30 minutes will not produce route cards on real tour days; they will set out without a card or with an incomplete card, which compromises every subsequent practice from D8 onwards.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D9.2 (slow but accurate; or fast but vague), do not push on to D9.3 and D9.4. Recycle the underlying skill that is slowing them or recycle the under-description discipline. The contingency layer in D9.3 and D9.4 assumes the candidate can produce a complete card reliably; without that, the contingency layer becomes paperwork rather than the integrative discipline it is meant to be.

The under-description issue (vague key-terrain and avalanche-assessment entries) is the single most common D9.2 failure mode. Coach for specificity at every weak entry; vague cards are worse than no cards because they create false confidence.
