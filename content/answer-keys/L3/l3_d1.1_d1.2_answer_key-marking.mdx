The compass-as-instrument pages are about familiarity, not technique. The technique pages start at D2.1. If a candidate is fast and confident on D1.1 and D1.2 they will move smoothly into D2; if they are still confused about which arrow is which, the D2 procedures will compound the confusion.

If a candidate names the parts correctly but cannot read a bearing within 2 degrees, the issue is usually compass-holding rather than knowledge. Run the Exercise 4 drill in person and watch the candidate's grip, tilt, and proximity to interference sources.

The 2-degrees-in-60-seconds standard is the IMS / EMS working target referenced in the IMS Nav Training Programme. Hold it from the start; a candidate who reads at 5 degrees of error in D1.2 will not meet the standard at D2.1, and the corrective work compounds the further into Level 3 the slip persists.
