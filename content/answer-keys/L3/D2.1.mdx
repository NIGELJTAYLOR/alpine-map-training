### Exercise 1 - Take five bearings

For trainer reference, the standard Section D2.1 extract is configured with five legs as follows:

| Leg | From | To | Grid bearing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Col du Grand Plan (2,720 m) | Refuge des Ecrins (2,450 m) | 218 |
| 2 | Refuge des Ecrins (2,450 m) | Lac Bleu (2,210 m) | 045 |
| 3 | Lac Bleu (2,210 m) | Pointe Sud (2,580 m) | 312 |
| 4 | Pointe Sud (2,580 m) | Col Nord (2,640 m) | 178 |
| 5 | Col Nord (2,640 m) | Cabane Vieille (2,300 m) | 003 |

(The named features are placeholders for the trainer's actual extract; substitute the equivalent named features for whatever venue the trainer is using.)

Mark each bearing within 2 degrees of the correct value. A candidate consistently more than 5 degrees off has slipped on the four-step procedure; identify which step is wrong by watching them take a bearing in person.

### Exercise 2 - Cardinal cross-check

For the standard extract:

| Leg | Grid bearing | Closest cardinal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 218 | SW |
| 2 | 045 | NE |
| 3 | 312 | NW |
| 4 | 178 | S |
| 5 | 003 | N |

Mark for the cardinal correctness. A candidate who calls 218 "SSW" or "south-west" is both acceptable; the cross-check is about the rough direction, not the exact label. A candidate who calls 218 "NE" has the 180-degree error and the bearing was probably taken with the direction-of-travel arrow pointing the wrong way.

### Exercise 3 - Diagnose the error

For the worked scenario (col at the top, hut at the south-west, candidate reads 042 degrees):

1. **No, the bearing is not correct.** A south-west direction is approximately 225 degrees, not 042.
2. **Most likely error: the direction-of-travel arrow was pointing from the hut to the col (uphill), not from the col to the hut (downhill).** 042 + 180 = 222, which is close to the expected 225, confirming the 180-degree flip.
3. **Correct bearing: approximately 222 degrees** (or whatever the candidate measures correctly with the direction-of-travel arrow pointing the right way).

Mark for the diagnosis (the 180-degree flip), not just for the corrected number. A candidate who notices the discrepancy but cannot diagnose the cause has not internalised the four-step procedure properly.

### Exercise 4 - Speed drill

For trainer reference, the standard speed-drill bearings are:

| Leg | Grid bearing | Target time |
|---|---|---|
| 6 | 094 | under 60 seconds |
| 7 | 271 | under 60 seconds |
| 8 | 156 | under 60 seconds |

Mark bearings within 2 degrees and times under 60 seconds as fully successful. A candidate at 90 seconds with correct bearings is on track; one at 60 seconds with bearings 5 degrees off has prioritised speed over accuracy and is not yet at the standard. The IMS / EMS target is both inside the limits, not one or the other.
