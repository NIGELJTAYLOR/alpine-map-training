The D10 pair closes Level 3. After this pair the candidate has the full Level 3 skill set demonstrated under sustained narration.

The D10.1 quiz marks fastest as a quiz; the D10.2 virtual day takes the longest. Build the time budget accordingly.

For D10.1, the error log should drive the action between sessions. After marking, sit with the candidate and translate each error-log entry into a specific commitment ("redo D2.1 Exercise 1 by Friday"; "complete the 7-day day log if not already done").

For D10.2, the diagnostic is the integration. A candidate who can produce strong individual elements (route card, instrument plan, criteria, escape route, "what if" response) but cannot weave them into a coherent narration has the components but not the practice. Treat D10.2 as the rehearsal: the candidate who can deliver a six-leg narration smoothly is the candidate the IMS / EMS assessor will see in the field.

The Level 4 readiness check at R4 is the closing artefact of Level 3. Record the answer in the trainer manual's progress log alongside the D10.1 error log; both go into the candidate's IMS / EMS preparation portfolio.

If the candidate's R4 is "yes" and the evidence supports it, the workbook has done its job for this candidate. Hand them over to the next phase of the IMS / EMS pathway: real tours, scenario response with a real group, the practical drill days, and the assessment course itself.

If the candidate's R4 is "not quite" or "no", recycle the relevant material and re-attempt D10.2 in a few weeks. Level 3 is the foundation for everything from here; do not push on if it is not solid.
