### Exercise 1 - Walk through the procedure

There is no fixed answer; the work depends on the candidate's chosen scenario. Mark by checking that:

- all eight steps are applied in order,
- step 3 (last confirmed position and elapsed time) is specific (a named feature and a specific minute count, not a vague "a while ago"),
- step 4 (fall-line bearing) uses the D7.2 aspect-of-slope procedure correctly,
- step 5 (match to map) constrains the position to a specific slope using the perpendicular-to-contour direction,
- step 6 (altimeter) refines to a unique point on that slope,
- step 7 (visible feature) cross-checks if any feature is available,
- and step 8 (next leg plan) resumes the route from the new fix.

A candidate who skips steps or executes them in the wrong order has not internalised the procedure. Coach the systematic execution.

### Exercise 2 - The leadership communication

| Candidate communication | Acceptable? | Better alternative if not |
|---|---|---|
| "We're a bit lost." | **No** | "I am temporarily unsure of position. I am stopping to relocate." |
| "I am temporarily unsure of position. I am stopping to relocate." | **Yes** | n/a |
| "Hold on, let me check the map while we walk." | **No** | "Stop. I am checking position. Please stay together at this point." |
| "The last confirmed position was the col 20 minutes ago. I expect to relocate within a few minutes." | **Yes** | n/a |
| "I think we should keep going and we'll figure it out." | **No** | "I am stopping to confirm position. I will not move further until I have a fix." |

Mark for the right judgement on each. A candidate who accepts the unprofessional communications has not internalised the leadership dimension; coach the language patterns.

### Exercise 3 - Why "temporarily unsure" works

A strong answer should make two points:

1. **For the candidate's own thinking:** "temporarily unsure" frames the situation as a managed problem with a known process. "Lost" frames it as a crisis with no plan. The mental framing affects the response.
2. **For the group's confidence:** "temporarily unsure" plus a stated action ("I am stopping to relocate") tells the group that the leader has a plan. "Lost" tells the group that there is no plan.

Bonus point: "temporarily unsure" is also more accurate. A candidate who knows the last confirmed position and has the tools to relocate is not "lost"; they are temporarily uncertain about a specific question (where am I now?) for which they have a procedure.

### Exercise 4 - The most common error

There is no fixed correct answer; common errors include:

- **Continuing to walk while consulting the map.** The most common error. Walking moves the candidate further from the last known position and makes relocation harder. The fix is the explicit "stop" instruction at step 1.
- **Avoiding the relocation entirely** by hoping the next feature will resolve the uncertainty. Sometimes this works (the next feature is recognisable); often it does not (the candidate continues on a wrong bearing for another 20 minutes before admitting the uncertainty).
- **Skipping the leadership communication.** The candidate runs the procedure silently and the group becomes anxious; the procedure works but the leadership impression is poor.
- **Trusting the GPS over the map-and-compass procedure.** The candidate skips the systematic relocation and just reads the GPS coordinates. This works in the moment but does not build the skill.

Mark for the candidate's specific identification and consequence reasoning.
