The D2 triplet is the foundational technique block of Level 3. Every later technique builds on it. If a candidate is wobbly on D2.1 (taking from the map), or D2.2 (variation), or D2.3 (walking), do not move on to D3 until the wobbly page is solid.

The most common compounded error is grid-bearing-correct, variation-correct, but walking-on-magnetic-bearing-with-grid-bearing in the field. The candidate has done the conversion on paper but has set the compass bezel to the grid bearing rather than the magnetic bearing. A useful check: before walking, ask the candidate "what bearing is on the bezel right now?" The right answer is always the magnetic bearing for that leg.

The 60-seconds-per-bearing target on D2.1 is the assessable speed standard from the IMS Nav Training Programme. The 15 m per 100 m drift target on D2.3 is the assessable accuracy standard. Both should be in the candidate's working repertoire by the end of Batch 11; the rest of Level 3 assumes them.

If a candidate cannot meet either standard after several attempts, recycle D1.2 (compass holding) and D2.1 (the four-step procedure). Most accuracy problems trace back to compass-holding habits rather than to the bearing technique itself.
