The D8 triplet is the application of all the prior Level 3 instruments to the specific challenge of poor visibility. After D8 the candidate has the poor-visibility toolkit complete: the whiteout sequence (D8.1), the pole-and-cord for hazardous extremes (D8.2), and the systematic relocation procedure (D8.3).

The IMS / EMS-relevant moments across the triplet:

- **D8.1:** the whiteout sequence is the standard procedure; the route card is the foundation.
- **D8.2:** the pole-and-cord is the technique to know but rarely deliberately use; conditional logic.
- **D8.3:** "temporarily unsure" is the leadership language; the systematic procedure is the skill.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D8, the typical pattern is:

- **Strong on D8.1 but weak on D8.3:** the candidate can execute the whiteout sequence but cannot recover when something goes wrong. Recycle D8.3.
- **Strong on D8.3 but weak on D8.1:** the candidate knows what to do when uncertain but has not internalised the prevention discipline of using the route card during a visibility drop. Recycle D8.1.
- **Knows D8.2 too well:** the candidate over-applies pole-and-cord when simpler techniques would be preferable. Coach the conditional use.

Do not push on to D9 (route cards) until D8 is solid. D9 will assume that the candidate sees the route card as the foundation of poor-visibility navigation; without that foundation, D9 becomes paperwork rather than the integrative artefact it is meant to be.
