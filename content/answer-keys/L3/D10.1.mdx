### Question 1 - Compass parts

Correct answer: the **direction-of-travel arrow** and the **orienting arrow**. The direction-of-travel arrow is fixed on the baseplate and points where the candidate is going; the orienting arrow is on the floor of the rotating bezel and is the alignment target for the magnetic needle.

A candidate who names other parts (e.g. magnetic needle and orienting arrow) has the wrong distinction. Recycle D1.1.

### Question 2 - Reading a bearing

Accuracy: **within 2 degrees**. Time: **under 60 seconds**. Both from the IMS Nav Programme assessment standard. Mark for both halves.

### Question 3 - The four-step procedure

Correct answers (single phrase per step):

1. Place the baseplate edge along the line from start to destination (direction-of-travel arrow towards destination).
2. Rotate the bezel until the parallel orienting lines align with the grid lines on the map.
3. Take the compass off the map.
4. Read the bearing from the direction-of-travel arrow against the bezel scale.

Mark for the right four steps in the right order. A candidate who omits step 1 ("baseplate edge along the line") or who reverses the direction of the arrow has the most consequential error; coach D2.1.

### Question 4 - Magnetic variation

Grid bearing 178 + variation +2 east = magnetic bearing **180** degrees.

A candidate who subtracts (178 - 2 = 176) has the rule reversed for east variation. Recycle D2.2.

### Question 5 - Walking on a bearing

Drift target: **less than 15 m per 100 m of travel**. From the IMS Nav Programme working standard.

### Question 6 - Back bearing

Forward 220 magnetic, subtract 180 (because forward is 180 or more): **back bearing 040 magnetic**.

A candidate who adds (220 + 180 = 400, which would wrap to 040 anyway via 400-360) gets the same answer by a different route; mark correct. A candidate who returns 400 or some other figure outside 0-360 has not applied the wrap rule.

### Question 7 - Resection

Minimum features: **two**. Optimal angular separation: **approximately 90 degrees**. Two features at 90 degrees produce a sharp intersection; closer-together or near-opposite features produce a shallow intersection where small bearing errors become large position errors.

### Question 8 - Bearing shapes drill

Closure error standard: **less than 5 paces** for IMS / EMS working standard. Mark for the right band; if the candidate gives a higher figure, coach the standard.

### Question 9 - Magnetic interference

Correct answers (sources and minimum safe distances):

| Source | Minimum safe distance |
|---|---|
| Steel ski bindings | 50 cm |
| Ice axes | 50 cm |
| Mobile phones | 30 cm |
| Radio handsets | 30 cm |

Mark for all four sources with the right distance for each. The 50 cm vs 30 cm distinction is the steel-vs-electronics distinction; a candidate who reverses them has the principle wrong.

### Question 10 - Altimeter and pressure

Rule of thumb: 1 hPa = approximately **8 m** of apparent altitude change. From D5.1; the standard rule for the lower troposphere.

### Question 11 - Barometric trend bands

Correct answers (trend and rate threshold):

| Trend | Rate threshold |
|---|---|
| Rising | Any positive rate |
| Steady | Less than 0.1 hPa/hr sustained for 3 hours |
| Slow fall | Less than 0.5 hPa/hr (falling) |
| Moderate fall | 0.5 to 1.0 hPa/hr (falling) |
| Rapid fall | More than 1.0 hPa/hr (falling) |

Mark for all five categories with the right thresholds. The most common confusion is between moderate and rapid; drill the threshold.

### Question 12 - Aspect-of-slope relocation

Stage 1 action: **take a compass bearing directly down the fall line of the slope you are standing on** (the aspect bearing).

Target time: under **two minutes** for the full procedure on a known slope.

Mark for both halves.

### Question 13 - Pole-and-cord conditional use

Correct answers (the three conditions, all required):

1. Visibility is less than 5 to 10 m.
2. The terrain has hidden hazards (drops, cornices, crevasse margins, sudden steepening).
3. A bearing-walked approach is the only available navigation option.

A candidate who names only one or two has missed the conditional logic; coach D8.2.

### Question 14 - Route card fields

Correct answers (all eleven):

1. Leg number
2. Start point and altitude
3. End point and altitude
4. Compass bearing (magnetic)
5. Distance (map)
6. Estimated time
7. Altimeter trigger height
8. Key terrain features
9. Avalanche assessment
10. Decision point criteria
11. Escape route

Mark for all eleven. A candidate missing two or more has not internalised the field set; recycle D9.1.

### Question 15 - Pre-agreed criteria standard

Correct answers (the three components):

1. A specific trigger (a measurable condition).
2. A specific action (turn around, take alternative line, etc.).
3. A named decision point (where the criterion is checked).

Mark for all three. A candidate missing any component has not internalised the standard; recycle D6.2 and D9.3.

### Confidence score and error log

There is no fixed answer; mark by checking the candidate has scored each skill area honestly and that the scores correlate with their performance on the relevant questions. The error log should name specific pages to revisit and specific actions to take.
