The D5.1 / D5.2 pair is about understanding the altimeter as an instrument and building the calibration habit. Both are foundational; D5.3 (drift management) and D5.4 (primary-tool use) assume the candidate has both.

The 24-hour stationary drill in D5.1 Exercise 1 is the experiment that lands the drift concept. A candidate who has not done the drill cannot really feel that an altimeter reading "altitude" can change while the altimeter is still on the desk. Insist on the drill before moving to D5.3.

The reliable-known-points discipline in D5.2 Exercise 3 is the working tool. A candidate who calibrates at unreliable points is introducing systematic errors; the recalibration habit becomes the source of the problem rather than its solution. Drill the discipline.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D5.2 (unsure about reliable points or about the recalibration cadence), do not push on to D5.3. The drift-as-weather-signal use in D5.3 assumes a confident calibration habit; without it the drift signal becomes noise.
