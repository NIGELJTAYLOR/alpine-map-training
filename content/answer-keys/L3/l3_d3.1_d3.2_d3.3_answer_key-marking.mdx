The D3 triplet is the field-side complement of the D2 triplet. A candidate who is fluent on D2 but slow on D3 has not yet internalised the inverse-direction skill (compass-on-ground, then to map, rather than compass-on-map, then to ground). Drill the inverse direction explicitly; both directions matter at IMS / EMS level.

Resection is taught at every level of mountain navigation but is the technique most commonly forgotten by candidates working in alpine environments, because alpine poor visibility makes it impractical so often. Hold the resection skill anyway; it is part of the IMS / EMS toolkit and the assessor will probe.

The aspect-of-slope alternative covered in D7.2 is the alpine-specific answer to "what do you do when resection does not work?" Make sure the candidate knows both techniques and can choose between them based on conditions.
