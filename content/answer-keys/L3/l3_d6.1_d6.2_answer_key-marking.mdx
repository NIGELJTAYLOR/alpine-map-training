The D6 pair is short on technique and heavy on judgement. The technique (reading pressure, classifying trend) is straightforward; the judgement (writing criteria, applying the framework) is where the IMS / EMS-relevant skill lies.

The three-day pressure log in D6.1 Exercise 1 is the practical drill that builds the trend-reading instinct. A candidate who has not done it will be guessing at trend classification rather than measuring; insist on the drill.

The pre-agreed criteria habit in D6.2 is one of the IMS / EMS-relevant habits that distinguishes a candidate from a strong candidate. The route card with explicit criteria is the artefact; the practice of writing them is the discipline. Mark Exercise 2 carefully and coach for specificity at every weak criterion.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D6 (vague trend classifications or vague criteria), do not push on to D7. The combined techniques in D7 are technical and assume the judgement framework from D6 is in place; without it, the candidate has techniques but no decision discipline.
