The D5.3 / D5.4 pair completes the altimeter section. After D5 the candidate has the full altimeter skill set: theory (D5.1), calibration (D5.2), drift management (D5.3), and primary-tool use (D5.4).

The 7-day day log in D5.3 Exercise 1 is the most valuable practical drill in the D5 section. It cannot be done in a single session; it is genuinely 7 days of small daily observations. If the candidate has not done the drill, the trust calibration in Exercise 5 will be abstract rather than personal. Insist on the drill, even if it means delaying D5.3 marking by a week.

The altimeter-primary concept in D5.4 is the alpine-specific framing that distinguishes the IMS / EMS skill set from generic land navigation. A candidate who can flip between compass-primary and altimeter-primary modes and articulate the choice on the route card is at the working assessment standard.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D5.4 (unsure about which legs are altimeter-primary, or unsure about the aim-high strategy), do not push on to D6. The barometer work in D6 builds on the altimeter-as-weather-signal use established in D5.3; the integrated D7 techniques assume confident D5 fluency.
