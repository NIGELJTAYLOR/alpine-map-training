The D9.3 / D9.4 pair completes the route card. After this pair the candidate has the full integrative artefact: basic fields (D9.1), full writing (D9.2), decision points (D9.3), and escape routes (D9.4).

The IMS / EMS-relevant moments across the pair:

- **D9.3:** the three-component criteria standard (trigger, action, location). This is the IMS / EMS-assessable discipline; vague criteria are coaching opportunities.
- **D9.4:** the no-viable-escape recognition. The candidate who can recognise when the route choice itself is wrong (because no leg has a viable escape) has the meta-planning skill the assessor expects.

If a candidate is borderline at the end of D9, the typical patterns are:

- **Strong on bearings and times but weak on criteria:** the candidate can produce the technical card but has not engaged with the contingency thinking. Recycle D6.2 and D9.3.
- **Strong on criteria but weak on escape routes:** the candidate has the criteria discipline but cannot identify alternative routes. Recycle C6.2 and D9.4.
- **Skipping the no-viable-escape question:** the candidate's tour has no flagged legs but should have. Press for honest review.

Do not push on to D10 (consolidation) until D9 is solid. D10 (the virtual day on a tour) exercises the full route card and instrument toolkit; without a complete D9, the D10 narration becomes a list of techniques rather than the integrated working response it is meant to be.
