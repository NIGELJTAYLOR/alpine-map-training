The fastest way to read a learner's level on this page is to time them through Exercise 1 with the answer table covered. A C2-fluent learner should complete the eight snippets in under three minutes with at most one sub-type error. A learner taking longer than five minutes, or making family-level errors, is not yet ready for Section C3.

If a learner is borderline, do not push on to C3 prematurely. Recycle the exercises that exposed the weakness. The cumulative effect of C2.1 to C2.4 is what makes Section C3 manageable; rushing the foundation makes the advanced features harder to learn, not easier.

The progression test for readiness to start C3 is:

- correct family on every snippet in Exercise 1,
- correct sub-type on at least six of the eight snippets,
- correct sketch pairing on at least six of the eight snippets in Exercise 2,
- a specific, actionable answer in Exercise 3,
- and an honest engagement with the stretch task in Exercise 4.

If all five hold, the learner is ready for C3.
