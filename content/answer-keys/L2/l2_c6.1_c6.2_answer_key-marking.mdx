The C6.1 / C6.2 pair is the closest the workbook gets to a real IMS / EMS in-field decision. Mark accordingly: the reasoning is the assessable skill, not the specific line.

For C6.1, the diagnostic is whether the learner can hold gradient inside the comfortable skinning band as a deliberate constraint rather than as an afterthought. A learner who picks line 3 because "zig-zags are how skinning works" has not internalised the constraint; a learner who picks line 3 because the average gradient is 9° has.

For C6.2, the diagnostic is whether the learner reads terrain configurations rather than individual features. A 30° slope is not a terrain trap; a 30° slope above a cliff band is. The combination is what produces consequence. A learner who identifies individual steep slopes without naming the configuration has done half the work.

If a learner is borderline on either page, do not move on to Section C7. The C7 virtual terrain walk integrates the C6 skills with everything earlier; weak C6 produces weak C7.
