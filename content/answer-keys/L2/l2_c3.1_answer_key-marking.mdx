The most common error on this page is confusion between cols and the bottom of a re-entrant or valley head. The check is the **two opposite directions** rule: a col rises on two opposite sides and falls on the other two; a re-entrant or valley head rises on three sides and falls on only one. If a learner is unsure, ask them to mark all four directions of slope on the page.

The route-choice exercise is the IMS / EMS-relevant skill on the page. Do not let learners answer it abstractly. Push for a specific direction, a specific climbing saving, and a specific reason. On the assessor's route card, a col choice has a number against it (the col's elevation), a saved-climbing figure against it, and a reason in plain English.

The C2.4 stretch task (snippet 9) was a deliberate first encounter with this contour pattern. If the learner sketched snippet 9, return to that sketch with them now and run a before-and-after comparison. The sketch they produced before they had the vocabulary is usually a better learning artefact than they expect; reinforcing what they already saw correctly (and gently noting what they missed) is more durable than re-teaching from scratch.
