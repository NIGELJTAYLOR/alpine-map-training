C7.1 marks fastest as a quiz; C7.2 takes the time. Build the time budget accordingly.

The error log from C7.1 should drive the action between sessions. After marking, sit with the learner and translate each error-log entry into a specific commitment ("redo C2.3 Exercise 1 by Tuesday"; "do five fall-line readings on a fresh extract before next session"). The commitment is what makes the log useful.

C7.2 is the closing rehearsal of Level 2. Treat it as such: the learner who narrates fluently and self-criticises honestly is ready for Level 3. The learner who narrates well but cannot identify any weakness is ready for Level 3 with a small caveat (genuinely strong learners do exist; check that the lack of weakness is real and not over-confidence). The learner who narrates poorly but feels ready is the one to slow down. Hold the readiness standard.

Level 3 readiness criteria, in summary:

- C7.1 quiz at 12 or more correct out of 15, with no family-level errors on the feature-identification questions.
- C7.2 narration with the four-part structure intact at all four stops, and route-choice reasoning at all three legs that references the comfortable skinning band, the bulletin, and the terrain-trap configuration where relevant.
- Honest reflection in R1, R2 and R3.
- An error log that the learner has acted on, or has a specific plan to act on.

If all four hold, the learner is ready. If any are weak, recycle the relevant material before progressing.
