The cross-section technique has a steep first attempt and a gentle second attempt: most learners find their first cross-section frustrating and their second much easier. Build that into the session shape: do C5.1 once for marking, then run a fresh section line and have the learner do it a second time as a self-check. The improvement is the learning artefact.

If a learner refuses to label the vertical exaggeration ("does it matter?"), insist. The IMS / EMS assessor expects to see the labelling. A cross-section without it can be misread by a third party and is not a reliable handover document.

If a learner reads slope angles off the visual angle of the cross-section drawing rather than off the contour spacing on the section line, that is the most important error to correct. The C1.2 rule of thumb is the angular truth; the cross-section is a visualisation of relief.

The cross-section is not the only way to visualise terrain (C5.2 and C5.3 cover the other approaches), but it is the technical anchor. After C5.1 the learner has a reliable, repeatable procedure for converting any contour pattern into a side view. That confidence should carry through the rest of Section C5 and into Section C6.
