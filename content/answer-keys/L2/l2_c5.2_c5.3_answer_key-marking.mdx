The C5.2 photo-matching is fastest to mark; the C5.3 sustained descriptions take longest. Build the time budget accordingly.

For C5.2, the trainer should aim to identify the learner's calibration biases. Common ones:

- mountains drawn too steep,
- bowls drawn too symmetrical,
- cliff bands drawn too geometric,
- ridges drawn too sharp.

These biases are predictable and persistent; once named they can be corrected on the next photograph the learner sees. Keep a brief note for each learner so the bias can be revisited.

For C5.3, the trainer should aim to build the structured voice as a habit. Some learners will resist the four-part structure as artificial; insist on it for the workbook even if the natural voice is freer. The structure is what the IMS / EMS assessor expects to hear in the field, and a learner who sounds structured under pressure is a learner who can pass the assessment.

If a learner's C5.3 descriptions are weak across all three exercises, do not move on to Section C6 until the structure is reliable. The route-thinking work in C6 builds on the structured-description voice; weak descriptions produce weak route narratives.
