For C3.2, the diagnostic is whether the learner reads the bowl as a single feature or as a structured one (headwall plus flanks plus floor). The structured reading is what supports avalanche-aware route choice; the single-feature reading does not.

For C3.3, the diagnostic is whether the learner reads contour spacing as a continuum (gentle to steep) or as containing two qualitatively different categories (walkable, including very steep, vs. impassable cliff). The qualitative break at merged contours plus rock symbology is the IMS / EMS-relevant distinction.

The C2.4 stretch task (snippet 10) was a deliberate first encounter with the cliff-band contour pattern. If the learner sketched snippet 10, return to that sketch with them now. Compare what they drew before they had the vocabulary with what they have just learned. As with the C3.1 col preview, this before-and-after comparison is more durable than re-teaching from scratch.

If the learner is borderline on either page, hold them at C3 before moving on to C4 (Aspect). Aspect reading depends on fluent terrain-shape recognition; weak shape recognition produces compounding errors at C4.
