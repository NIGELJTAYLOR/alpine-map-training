### Exercise 1 — Core definition

A contour line joins points of equal **height** above sea level.

### Exercise 2 — True or false

1. **True.** All points on one contour line are at the same elevation.
2. **False.** Roads and tracks are shown by their own line styles and colours, not by contour lines.
3. **True.** Reading contours is what reveals the shape of the ground.
4. **True.** The contour interval varies between maps and must always be checked from the legend.

### Exercise 3 — Short explanation

A strong answer should mention most of the following:

- contours show height,
- contours show the shape of the land,
- contours reveal steepness, valleys, ridges and climbs,
- contours are essential to mountain route choice and IMS-level navigation.

### Exercise 4 — Height sequence

1. Contour interval = 10 m.
2. Height gained from 200 m to 230 m = 30 m.
