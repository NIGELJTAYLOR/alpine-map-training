The single most common error on C4.1 is treating the fall line as parallel to the contour rather than perpendicular. The fix is the right-angle drill: hold a pencil at right angles to the contour line, and the pencil shows the fall line. Make this a habit before moving to C4.2.

On C4.2, the most common error is reading aspect at four directions (N, E, S, W) rather than eight. The avalanche bulletin uses eight, and so does the IMS / EMS assessor. Hold the eight-direction standard from the start.

The aspect-and-snow exercise (Exercise 3) is the page's most assessable IMS / EMS skill. A learner who can name the aspect but cannot reason about the snow has done half the work. Hold both halves.

The bulletin-to-terrain exercise (Exercise 4) is the integration. If a learner can do this exercise fluently they can do the route-card-thinking work that comes in Section C6.

If a learner is still wobbly at the end of these pages on either fall-line reading or aspect reading, recycle. Section C5 (visualisation) builds on aspect; weak aspect makes weak visualisation.
