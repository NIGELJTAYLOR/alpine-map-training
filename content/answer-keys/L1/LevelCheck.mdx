1. A topographic map is used to understand and navigate the land, including its shape, features and routes.
2. A bird’s-eye view shows the land from above, while a ground view is what you see from eye level on the terrain.
3. Any three of: title, legend/key, scale bar or scale statement, north arrow, grid lines and numbers.
4. Blue usually represents water.
5. Because symbols and styles can vary, and the legend confirms the correct meaning.
6. 250 metres.
7. 1 cm on a 1:50,000 map covers more ground than 1 cm on a 1:25,000 map.
8. Eastings first, then northings.
9. A grid square.
10. Because it helps the map match the terrain and reduces mental rotation.
11. East.
12. Because if the PDF has been printed at the wrong size, the original scale may no longer be accurate.

## Marking guide
- 10–12 correct: secure Level 1 understanding.
- 7–9 correct: mostly secure, but revisit weak areas.
- 0–6 correct: more Level 1 consolidation needed before progressing.
