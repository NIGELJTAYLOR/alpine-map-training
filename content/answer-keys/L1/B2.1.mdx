### Exercise 1 — Label the parts
This exercise is descriptive. Check that the learner has correctly identified positions on their actual map for:

1. Title or map name.  
2. Series name.  
3. Scale statement.  
4. Scale bar.  
5. Legend or key.  
6. North arrow.  
7. Grid lines.  
8. Grid numbers.

Locations will vary by map series, but each item should be present and correctly recognised.

### Exercise 2 — Function matching

Correct answers:

- 1. D (North arrow): shows the direction of north on the map.  
- 2. C (Scale bar): lets you convert centimetres on the map to distance on the ground.  
- 3. E (Grid lines and numbers): divide the map into squares for precise locations.  
- 4. A (Title): tells you which area the map covers.  
- 5. B (Legend/key): explains the meaning of symbols and colours.

### Exercise 3 — Quick questions

1. Why should you always check the scale when you pick up a map?  
   - Model idea: Because the scale tells you how distances on the map relate to real distances, and you may need to switch between 1:25,000 and 1:50,000.

2. Why should you always inspect the legend before using an unfamiliar map?  
   - Model idea: Because different maps use slightly different symbols, and the legend explains what they mean.

3. Why is it important to know where north is on the map?  
   - Model idea: Because orienting the map to north helps you match the map to the real terrain.

4. How do grid lines help with navigation?  
   - Model idea: They allow you to use grid references to give and find precise positions.
