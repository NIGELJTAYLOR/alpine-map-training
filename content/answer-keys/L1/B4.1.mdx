### Exercise 1 — Fill in the blanks

1. On a 1:25,000 map, 1 cm = 250 metres.
2. On a 1:50,000 map, 1 cm = 500 metres.
3. On a 1:25,000 map, 4 cm = 1 km.
4. On a 1:50,000 map, 2 cm = 1 km.

### Exercise 2 — Quick conversions

| Scale | Map distance | Ground distance |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1:25,000 | 2 cm | 500 m |
| 1:25,000 | 6 cm | 1.5 km |
| 1:50,000 | 3 cm | 1.5 km |
| 1:50,000 | 5 cm | 2.5 km |

### Exercise 3 — Which map would you choose?
Model answers:

1. 1:25,000 is usually better for detailed route-finding because it shows more fine detail.
2. 1:50,000 is more useful for seeing a wider area quickly because it covers more ground on one sheet.
3. A navigator may use both because one gives overview and the other gives detailed local information.

### Exercise 4 — Explain it simply
A strong answer should explain that scale means reduction from real world to map, and should correctly describe the practical differences between 1:25,000 and 1:50,000.
