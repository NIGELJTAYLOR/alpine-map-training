### Exercise 1 — Legend lookup
This depends on the learner’s map. Check that each requested feature has been found in the legend and that the learner’s sketch broadly resembles the printed symbol.

### Exercise 2 — Symbol hunt on the map
Answers vary. The important check is whether the learner has found real examples of:

- a building or hut,
- a path or route,
- a water feature,
- a vegetation feature,
- and a steep or rocky feature.

### Exercise 3 — What does this tell you?
Model answers:

1. A bridge symbol crossing a blue line suggests there is a crossing point over a stream or river.
2. A hut near a path junction is useful because it is often a distinct feature that can help confirm position.
3. Cliff or steep-ground symbols should make the learner think carefully about safety, route choice and whether the line is practical.

### Exercise 4 — Tiny map-square description
A strong answer should use multiple clues together, for example:

- open land or woodland,
- a stream or path,
- a building or hut,
- and some sense of terrain from contours or steep-ground symbols.
