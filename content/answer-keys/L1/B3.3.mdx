### Exercise 1 — Legend comparison table
Answers vary depending on the two maps used. A strong answer should show:

- that the learner has actually compared the two legends,
- that they noticed both similarities and differences,
- and that they are becoming more careful about unfamiliar symbols.

### Exercise 2 — What surprised you?
This is reflective. Good responses usually identify:

- at least two genuine differences in symbol style, line type, label or colour,
- and at least one broad similarity, such as blue still representing water or brown still representing contours.

### Exercise 3 — Why does this matter?
A strong answer should mention most of the following:

- symbols can vary between map series or countries,
- assumptions can lead to misreading the terrain or route,
- the legend is the reliable source for exact meaning,
- checking the legend reduces navigation errors.

### Exercise 4 — Personal rule
A good answer should show that the learner intends to check the legend before trusting unfamiliar symbols.
