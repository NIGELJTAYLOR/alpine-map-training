### Exercise 1 — Colour matching
Correct answers:

- Brown = B (Terrain / contours)
- Blue = A (Water)
- Black = D (Man-made features)
- Green = C (Vegetation / woodland)
- White = E (Open land)

### Exercise 2 — Colour scan task
Answers will vary by map. Strong responses should show that the learner can use colour as a first-pass interpretation. Look for ideas such as:

- Brown: steepness, hills, ridges, valleys, terrain complexity.
- Blue: streams, lakes, drainage lines, wet ground, glacier areas.
- Green/white: forest vs open ground, dense vegetation vs clearer land.
- Black: huts, buildings, tracks, roads, boundaries, infrastructure.

### Exercise 3 — Quick interpretation
Model answers:

1. White area likely represents open ground.
2. Dark green block likely represents woodland or dense vegetation.
3. Blue line represents a stream or river.
4. Black rectangles likely represent buildings or huts.
5. Many brown contours tell you the land has shaped terrain and likely changing height.

### Exercise 4 — Legend check
This is reflective. Good answers should show:

- one case where the learner correctly guessed the meaning from colour,
- one case where the legend provided more precise information than colour alone.
