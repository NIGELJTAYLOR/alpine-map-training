## Purpose of this section
These notes support the next stage of Level 1, where the learner develops confidence with colours, symbols, legends, scale and grid references. The aim is to move from passive recognition to active use.

## Coaching priorities
Focus on helping the learner:

1. read colour as a quick first impression,
2. use the legend as a reliable checking tool,
3. recognise that map styles vary,
4. understand scale as a practical tool rather than a mathematical abstraction,
5. prepare for precise location work using grids.

## Coaching notes for B3.1–B3.3
At this stage, the learner should move beyond simply reciting colour meanings. They should begin to say things like:

- “This broad white area suggests open ground.”
- “This green block likely means woodland.”
- “I should check the legend before assuming exactly what this path symbol means.”

Useful prompts:

- What is your first impression from colour alone?
- What did the legend confirm?
- What changed when you compared two different maps?
- Which symbol or line style might have tricked you if you had not checked the legend?

## Coaching notes for B4.1
Scale often feels abstract at first, so keep it practical.

Useful prompts:

- If I measure 1 cm, what does that mean on the ground?
- Which map would help more if I needed fine route detail?
- Which map would help more for broader planning?

Encourage the learner to memorise:

- 1:25,000 → 1 cm = 250 m
- 1:50,000 → 1 cm = 500 m

These quick conversions reduce cognitive load later.

## Typical learner difficulties
Common issues include:

- remembering colour meanings but not using them actively,
- forgetting that different legends vary,
- confusing “larger scale” with “larger area”,
- and making avoidable arithmetic mistakes in simple distance conversions.

## Marking guidance
Mark for:

- correct recognition of colours and symbols,
- appropriate use of the legend,
- sound understanding of why comparison matters,
- and practical understanding of scale.

If the learner explains scale clearly in plain English, that is a strong sign of understanding.

## Readiness to move on
The learner is ready to progress toward the next Level 1 sections when they can:

- use colours to make sensible first judgements,
- confirm symbols using the legend,
- explain why different maps need checking,
- and convert simple distances confidently at 1:25,000 and 1:50,000.
