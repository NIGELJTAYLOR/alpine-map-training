## Purpose of this section
These notes support the beginning and end of Level 1 as a coherent module rather than just a sequence of isolated pages.

## Using the contents page
The contents page should be used to show the learner the scope of Level 1 before starting. This helps set expectations and shows that the level is building from basic map understanding toward practical printed-map use.

## Using the reflection page
The reflection page should be used after the Level 1 check quiz and after one short recap discussion or review. The aim is to help the learner identify both strengths and remaining weak points before entering Level 2.[web:277][web:279][web:284]

## Reflection prompts
If the learner struggles to reflect in writing, use spoken prompts such as:

- What now feels much easier than it did at the start?
- What still makes you hesitate?
- What mistake do you notice less often now?
- Which skill should be practised once more before moving on?

## Progression decision
A learner does not need perfect fluency before moving on, but they should show secure understanding of Level 1 foundations. If they remain shaky on scale, grids or basic orientation, give one more consolidation session before starting Level 2.[web:277][web:279][web:285]
