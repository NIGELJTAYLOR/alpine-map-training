## Purpose of this section
These trainer notes support the transition from general map familiarity into practical map use. The learner should begin to measure distances, understand print-scale risks on extracts, and use grid systems confidently.

## Coaching priorities
The trainer should focus on helping the learner:

1. move from knowing scale to using scale,
2. measure both straight and curved route lengths,
3. recognise that printed extracts and PDFs must be checked for correct print size,
4. understand the order of eastings and northings,
5. give reliable four-figure grid references.

## Coaching notes for B4.2–B4.3
Keep the scale work practical and visual.

Useful prompts:

- Is this a straight line or a curved route?
- What tool would help here: ruler or string?
- Have you checked the scale statement or scale bar?
- Is this printed at the correct size?
- What is the quickest conversion rule for this scale?

For printed extracts, remind the learner that a PDF may have been reduced or enlarged. If in doubt, use the printed scale bar to confirm the effective scale.

## Common mistakes in scale work
Typical errors include:

- forgetting which scale is in use,
- confusing 1:25,000 with 1:50,000,
- measuring a curved route with a straight ruler line,
- and trusting a resized printout without checking the scale bar.

## Coaching notes for B5.1–B5.2
The grid system should be taught with repetition and consistency.

Useful prompts:

- Which lines run vertically?
- Which lines run horizontally?
- Which do we say first?
- What square are we in, not what exact point?

Use the same phrasing repeatedly:

- **Eastings first.**
- **Then northings.**
- **Left line first, bottom line second.**

## Marking guidance
Mark for:

- correct use of scale rules,
- sensible choice of measuring method,
- awareness of print-scaling issues,
- correct easting/northing order,
- and accurate four-figure references.

If the learner gets a reference wrong, check whether the problem is understanding the square, reversing the order, or reading the wrong line.

## Readiness to move on
The learner is ready for the next stage when they can:

- measure simple straight and curved distances on printed extracts,
- convert common measurements quickly,
- explain why print scaling matters,
- identify eastings and northings correctly,
- and give dependable four-figure grid references.
