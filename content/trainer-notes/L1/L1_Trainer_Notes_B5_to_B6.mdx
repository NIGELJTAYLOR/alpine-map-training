## Purpose of this section
These notes support the final Level 1 topics: grid-reference fluency, directional awareness, map rotation and simple orienting. The aim is to move the learner from static knowledge into usable early navigation habits.

## Coaching priorities
Focus on helping the learner:

1. give four-figure references consistently,
2. use north, south, east and west correctly,
3. understand why rotating the map helps,
4. begin orienting the paper to the real world using obvious features,
5. review Level 1 knowledge through a short check quiz.

## Coaching notes for B5.3
Keep the drill brisk and repetitive. The learner should begin to answer four-figure references with less hesitation.

Useful prompts:

- What is the square?
- Which number comes first?
- Are you reading the left line or the right line?
- Are you reading the bottom line or the top line?

If errors occur, slow the task down and return to very simple labelled grids.

## Coaching notes for B6.1
At this stage, keep direction language simple and consistent.

Useful prompts:

- If north is at the top, what is to the right?
- What is above this feature on the page?
- What is below it?

Make sure the learner understands that turning the paper in their hand does not alter the true directional meaning of the printed map itself.

## Coaching notes for B6.2–B6.3
Map rotation and simple orienting often unlock confidence very quickly.

Useful prompts:

- Which feature is easiest to match first?
- If the stream on the ground runs that way, how should the map be turned?
- Does the paper now match the landscape better than before?
- What one clue are you trusting most right now?

Use strong, obvious features first: streams, roads, valley lines, woodland edges, settlement edges or tracks.

## Common difficulties
Typical problems include:

- reversing grid-reference order,
- mixing up east and west,
- thinking the map's north changes when the page is turned,
- and trying to orient from too many weak clues at once.

## End-of-Level-1 check
Use the short quiz as a quick confidence and readiness check. It is not meant to be a high-pressure test; it is a structured review.

A learner who scores strongly but still feels hesitant may benefit from one more printed-extract practice session before progressing.

## Readiness to progress
The learner is ready for Level 2 when they can:

- give reliable four-figure grid references,
- use north, south, east and west correctly,
- explain why map rotation helps,
- orient a simple printed extract using obvious features,
- and pass the Level 1 check with broadly secure understanding.
