### Learning aim

By the end of this page, you should be able to follow a route across a 1:25,000 alpine map and produce a sustained narrative of the terrain at each stop, the route choice between stops, and the changes in conditions or hazards along the way. This is the closing exercise of Level 2 and the bridge into Level 3.

### Why this matters

The virtual terrain walk is the closest the workbook gets to a full route narration. It integrates:

- contour-shape recognition (Sections C2 to C3),
- steepness reading (C1.2),
- aspect reading (C4),
- visualisation (C5),
- route choice (C6.1),
- terrain-trap reading (C6.2),
- and the four-part description structure (C5.3).

The IMS / EMS assessor's most demanding in-field test is to ask the candidate to narrate a tour while moving across it. C7.2 is the rehearsal, on paper, with the trainer alongside.

### How to use this page

Working materials: the Section C7.2 map extract (provided by your trainer; 1:25,000, 10 m contours), with four points marked **A**, **B**, **C** and **D**, plus pre-sketched route lines connecting them.

The route runs **A → B → C → D**. You will narrate the terrain at each stop and the route choice between each pair of stops. The narration is in your own words; use the four-part description structure (underfoot, slope and aspect, near features, far features) for the stops, and the C6.1 line-comparison vocabulary for the legs.

Pace the exercise like a real tour: short narrations at each stop (3 to 5 sentences), short route-choice notes between stops (2 to 3 sentences each).

### Why this matters for IMS

C7.2 is the closing rehearsal of Level 2. Every component skill the IMS / EMS assessor will probe in the field has appeared somewhere in the walk, and the sustained narration is the form the assessor will hear it in. After C7.2 the workbook hands the learner over to Level 3 (compass bearings, altimeter, route card preparation, and beyond), confident that the contour-reading foundation is in place.

If the readiness check at R3 returns "not quite" or "no", that is the workbook working as intended. The error log from C7.1 plus the closing reflection here gives a clear list of what to do. Do that work before starting Level 3.
