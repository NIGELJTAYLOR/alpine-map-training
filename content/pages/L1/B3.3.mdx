### Learning aim
By the end of this page, the learner should be able to compare two different map legends, notice similarities and differences, and explain why checking the legend is essential whenever using an unfamiliar map.

### Why legend comparison matters
A topographic map is only as useful as your ability to decode it correctly. Even when two maps look broadly similar, the symbols, colours, line styles and labels may vary.

This means a good navigator never assumes. A good navigator checks.

### What to compare
When comparing two map legends, look especially for:

- paths and walking routes,
- roads and tracks,
- rivers and streams,
- huts or buildings,
- woodland or vegetation,
- cliffs or steep ground,
- lift systems or mountain infrastructure,
- and any symbols that are unfamiliar.

### Worked example
Imagine you place two maps side by side.

- On Map A, a walking route may be shown with a red dashed line.
- On Map B, a similar route may be shown with a black dotted line.
- On one map, a hut symbol may be a small black square.
- On another, it may have a different icon or label.

If you rely on assumption instead of checking the legend, you can misread the map.
