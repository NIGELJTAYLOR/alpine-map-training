### Learning aim
By the end of this page, the learner should be able to give and read four-figure grid references quickly and reliably on simple training grids and printed map extracts.

### Why this drill matters
Four-figure grid references are one of the first truly practical map skills. They help the learner move from “I can see the feature” to “I can state where it is”.

The key ideas remain the same each time:

- Find the correct square.
- Read the easting first.
- Read the northing second.

### Quick reminder
A four-figure grid reference identifies a **grid square**, not an exact point. The first two digits come from the left-hand grid line of the square. The second two digits come from the bottom grid line of the square.

### Exercise 1 — Fast square drill
Give the correct four-figure reference for each square.

1. Right of easting 14 and above northing 62 = __________
2. Right of easting 37 and above northing 45 = __________
3. Right of easting 09 and above northing 28 = __________
4. Right of easting 51 and above northing 73 = __________
5. Right of easting 26 and above northing 19 = __________
6. Right of easting 44 and above northing 56 = __________

### Exercise 3 — Spot and correct the mistake
A learner gives these answers:

- A hut in the square right of easting 32 and above northing 61 is written as **6132**.
- A bridge in the square right of easting 07 and above northing 43 is written as **0743**.
- A wall in the square right of easting 48 and above northing 25 is written as **2548**.

For each one, write whether it is **correct** or **incorrect**, and if incorrect write the corrected answer.
