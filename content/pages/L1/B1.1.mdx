### Learning aim
By the end of this page, the learner should be able to explain what a topographic map is, how it differs from a road map or satellite view, and why it is useful for mountain navigation.

### What is a topographic map?
A topographic map is a **detailed picture of the land seen from above**. It shows the shape of the ground, water features, vegetation, buildings, tracks and other important information in a carefully organised visual form.

Unlike a road map, it is not only interested in getting you from one town to another. It is designed to help you understand the land itself.

Unlike a satellite image, it does not simply show what the camera saw. Instead, it selects, simplifies and organises information so that you can read the land clearly and make decisions.

### Why this matters in mountain areas
In mountain terrain, the shape of the land matters just as much as distance. A route that looks short on a road map may cross steep ground, cliffs, gullies or ridges. A good mountain map helps the navigator understand:

- Where the high and low ground is.
- Which slopes are steep or gentle.
- Where rivers, huts, tracks and ridges are.
- Which route options are likely to be practical.

### Three ways of viewing the same landscape
You can think about maps using three different viewpoints:

1. **Road map view** – useful for roads and settlements, but poor for terrain shape.
2. **Satellite view** – useful for seeing real surfaces and patterns, but not always easy to interpret quickly.
3. **Topographic map view** – useful for understanding the structure of the terrain and navigating through it.

### Worked example
Imagine you need to move from a valley hamlet to a mountain hut.

- A road map may show the road to the valley, but not the steepness of the hillside.
- A satellite image may show forest, rock and paths, but it may still be hard to judge the shape of the slope quickly.
- A topographic map helps you see the route, the land shape and the key features together.

That is why topographic maps are the standard tool for mountain navigation.
