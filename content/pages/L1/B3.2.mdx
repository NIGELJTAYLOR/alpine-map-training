### Learning aim
By the end of this page, the learner should be able to find and interpret common topographic map symbols using the legend and real map extracts.

### Why symbols matter
Colour gives a fast overview, but symbols give precision. Small signs on the map may show a hut, bridge, path, wall, cliff, church, road, lift or many other features that matter for route choice.

The key habit is simple: **notice the symbol, then confirm it in the legend if needed**.

### A practical way to learn symbols
Do not try to memorise an entire legend in one sitting. Instead:

1. Learn a few high-value symbols first.
2. Find them on a real map.
3. Say aloud what they mean.
4. Revisit them several times across different map types.

### Core symbols to start with
Use your own map legend to identify symbols for:

- Building or hut.
- Footpath or walking route.
- Road or track.
- River or stream.
- Bridge.
- Woodland.
- Cliff or very steep ground.
