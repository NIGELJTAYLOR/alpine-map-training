### Learning aim
By the end of this page, the learner should understand why north is shown at the top of most maps and should be able to use north, south, east and west correctly on a printed extract.

### Why north matters
Maps need a consistent direction system. On most topographic maps, **north is at the top of the sheet**. Once the learner understands this, they can describe where features lie in relation to each other more clearly.

For example:

- if north is at the top,
- south is at the bottom,
- east is to the right,
- west is to the left.

### From paper to ground
When the map is not aligned with the ground, the learner may have to do a mental rotation. That is why good navigators often turn the map so it matches the world around them.

At this stage, the aim is simply to become confident with north and the other main directions.
