### Level 1 — Map Literacy Foundations

This level builds the essential map-reading habits needed before moving into terrain interpretation. It focuses on understanding how maps work, how to read their structure, how to use scale and grids, and how to orient a printed extract confidently.

### What success looks like at the end of Level 1

By the end of Level 1, the learner should be able to:

- explain what a topographic map is for,
- describe the difference between map view and ground view,
- find and use the main parts of a map,
- recognise the usual meaning of the main map colours,
- use the legend to interpret common symbols,
- understand and apply 1:25,000 and 1:50,000 scale,
- measure simple distances on printed extracts,
- give reliable four-figure grid references,
- and orient a simple printed map using obvious features.
