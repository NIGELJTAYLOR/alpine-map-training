**Used in:** D9.3 across the full route card. Worksheet for working out criteria before transferring them to the route card.

**How to use:** identify three to five decision points on the tour. For each, write at least one criterion meeting the three-component standard (specific trigger, specific action, named decision point). Use multiple trigger types across the criteria (time, weather, group condition, snow, terrain) rather than only one.

```
Trigger types to consider (use a variety, not all the same type):
- Time of day (e.g. "if past 10:30am at the col")
- Weather / barometer (e.g. "if pressure has fallen more than 2 hPa since departure")
- Group condition (e.g. "if any member needs more than 5 minutes' rest")
- Snow observation (e.g. "if fresh wind-loading visible above 2,600 m")
- Terrain-trap concern (e.g. "if visibility drops below 50 m above the cornice")

Three-component standard:
1. Specific trigger (measurable, not vague)
2. Specific action (turn around, take alternative line, abort)
3. Named decision point (the location where the criterion is checked)

Pre-agreed criteria are non-negotiable on the day. The decision was made at the desk.
```
